# Council review — 1781097621

**Problem:** How to teach a numerical PROCEDURE (QR by Householder, 3×3) to an ADHD visual learner so they can actually DO it — see the step-by-step + how each matrix number plugs into the formulas. Open sub-question: does showing the geometric reflection (column folds across the mirror-plane onto the e₁ axis) genuinely HELP a student, or just look interesting?
**Proposed approach:** Primary vehicle = an ARITHMETIC TRACE (each step shows formula — norm; v = x − αe₁; H = I − 2vvᵀ/vᵀv; apply — with the ACTUAL numbers substituted + computed live, reproducible by hand). The 3D geometric reflection = a SHORT intuition aside, not the main vehicle. Grid-of-changing-numbers (the failed first attempt) rejected.
**Context:** Single ADHD visual learner (loves 3B1B), Romanian, low confidence. A first QR attempt failed because it showed numbers mutating but not the mechanism. This becomes the template for ~20 numerical-methods lessons.
**Timestamp:** 2026-06-10 (epoch 1781097621)

## 🔴 Devil's Advocate
STANCE: CONDITIONAL
REASONING: The arithmetic trace commits the SAME sin as the failed grid — just at a different layer — IF it's passive. A column of substituted numbers the student READS rather than GENERATES is isomorphic to "watch the column fold": he follows along, feels competent, then fails cold on a blank sheet. The proposal outsources the real pedagogical question ("what must the student DO to encode this?") to a display-format choice. Arithmetic-vs-geometry is the wrong axis of choice.
KEY CONCERN: No retrieval/production step. A trace that's read, not generated, is a numeric spectacle replacing a geometric one. The fix isn't the format — it's adding active recall (predict-then-reveal each sub-result, blank-paper reconstruction, worked-example completion).

## 📚 Domain Expert
STANCE: APPROVE
REASONING: Matches the worked-example effect (Sweller & Cooper 1985; Kalyuga 2001) — for procedural numerical tasks novices learn far better from fully-worked symbolic-then-numeric traces than from animations. Trefethen & Bau structure Householder QR exactly this way (derive reflector, then walk a concrete 3×3 with every value). Rejecting the grid is correct — it triggers the split-attention effect. The 3D reflection's dual-coding role (Paivio) is correctly scoped: 3Blue1Brown and Strang use geometry for ONE clean intuition then anchor to arithmetic — never AS the procedure.
KEY CONCERN: The v-construction sign step (α = −sign(x₁)‖x‖, to avoid cancellation) must be explicit with real numbers — it's where hand-reproducibility collapses, and it's the one place geometry MISLEADS (the sign flip looks arbitrary geometrically but is numerically load-bearing). Name it as a sub-step, not a throwaway line.

## 🧱 First Principles
STANCE: CONDITIONAL
REASONING: The real goal isn't "understand Householder" — it's "reproduce it on a 3×3 by hand on an exam without panicking." That decomposes into two sub-skills: which formula to reach for when, and executing the arithmetic without losing track of which column. The arithmetic trace trains both; the 3D reflection trains neither (it answers a third question — "why geometrically" — nobody asked). But the trace must keep the PROCEDURE SKELETON dominant over the arithmetic.
KEY CONCERN: The trace collapses unless the procedure skeleton (4 named steps, same each column) is visually DOMINANT over the numbers — ADHD means the numbers eat attention and the skeleton vanishes, so the student memorizes one arithmetic instance instead of the reusable procedure.

## ⚠️ Risk Analyst
STANCE: CONDITIONAL
REASONING: Right instinct, but the ADHD failure mode is wall-of-substitution: four dense formula lines with decimals landing at once = attention collapse before any step lands. Second risk: template brittleness — a static LaTeX block means every future topic (Gram-Schmidt, LU…) degrades into the same wall with different symbols, shipping the cognitive-load problem not the lesson. The geometry is net-positive ONLY as a one-sentence anchor BEFORE the numbers, never interleaved (interleaving doubles working-memory demand). Decimal ugliness (−1.3333) → round to 4 sig figs and label the rounding.
KEY CONCERN: Pacing must be in the learner's hands — ONE formula line per interaction, the computed number appearing only AFTER the formula is shown, so attention is never split between "what is this formula" and "what do these numbers mean." Bake this into the template contract or every numerical-methods lesson silently regresses to the grid problem in new clothes.

CORE FINDING:
The arithmetic trace IS the right primary vehicle — the Domain Expert confirms it's exactly how Householder is taught well (Trefethen & Bau, Strang), and reproducibility is the real goal. But do NOT build it as specified, because a trace the student READS is isomorphic to the grid demo that just failed (Devil's Advocate) — both are watch-and-follow. The fix is unanimous in spirit: make it ACTIVE and LEARNER-PACED, with the reusable procedure skeleton dominant over the numbers.

AGENT CONSENSUS: 1 APPROVE, 3 CONDITIONAL — 0 flagged. Unanimous: right vehicle, but passive-as-specified.

KEY ISSUES:
- **Passive = re-failure (CRITICAL, Devil's Advocate).** Add active recall: predict-then-reveal each sub-result; the student computes a step before it's shown; end on a blank-slate "now you do the next column."
- **Pacing + no split-attention (Risk Analyst).** Reveal ONE formula line per click; the computed number appears only AFTER its formula. Never dump all four lines at once.
- **Skeleton over arithmetic (First Principles).** The 4 named steps (norm → build v → form H → apply), identical per column, must be the visually dominant, persistent frame; the numbers fill into it.
- **Name the sign step (Domain Expert).** α = −sign(x₁)‖x‖ gets its own labeled sub-step (it's where reproducibility dies AND where geometry misleads). Round decimals to 4 sig figs, label the rounding.

ON THE OWNER'S QUESTION (does geometry help, or just look interesting?):
Both. It genuinely HELPS — but ONLY as a single one-screen intuition anchor shown BEFORE the arithmetic ("we're folding the column onto the axis; length is preserved → that's why the entries below go to zero"), exactly how 3Blue1Brown and Strang use it. It becomes mere eye-candy — and actively harmful — the moment it's the main vehicle or runs interleaved/in-parallel with the numbers (it doubles working-memory load and misleads on the sign). So: keep the 3D reflection, demote it to a 1-screen "why it works" opener, then switch to the active arithmetic trace.

RECOMMENDED PATH:
Rebuild the QR lesson as: ① geometry anchor (the 3D reflection, one screen, "why the column lands on the axis") → ② an ACTIVE, learner-paced arithmetic trace where the persistent 4-step skeleton frames each column, one formula line reveals per click, each number appears after its formula, the sign step is named, and the student predicts at least one sub-result before it's shown → ③ "your turn: do column 2" with the skeleton as scaffold. Make the one-line-per-interaction + skeleton-dominant + active-recall rules the TEMPLATE CONTRACT for all numerical-methods lessons, or they all regress.
