## 🔴 Devil's Advocate
AGENT: Devil's Advocate
STANCE: CONDITIONAL
REASONING: The approach assumes "3Blue1Brown-grade visual intuition" is a renderer-styling property that a generic SVG layout shared by every figure in a family can deliver — that is the fatal assumption. 3B1B's quality comes from per-scene CHOREOGRAPHY: deliberate spatial composition, motion that traces the algorithm's reasoning, emphasis timing, and a layout custom-fit to THIS specific instance's data — none of which a single 480×360 family template, blind to instance semantics, can express; you will polish typography, spacing, and theme tokens to a clean-but-flat diagram and Alex will again say "still shit" because the gap was never aesthetics-at-the-renderer-level, it was instance-specific intuition design that verify-once structurally forbids. Worse, the only escape valve Alex controls is "drive the aesthetic," but he's the one person who has ALREADY rejected the output, can't articulate the subject pedagogy that would tell the renderer what to emphasize, and will be steering by gut on a template whose ceiling is capped below his target — so the self-see loop converges on the best-looking version of a structurally insufficient artifact and quietly relabels "we improved it" as "we hit the bar." The visual CI gate makes this dangerous: it proves no-overlap/no-clip/theme-conformance, which Alex will read as "the gate passed = it's good," laundering a polished-but-flat result through green CI when the gate cannot see "boring," "doesn't build intuition," or "wrong thing emphasized."
KEY CONCERN: Before any pixel work, prove (on the worst-judged family, e.g. sort/merge) that one instance-agnostic family renderer can reach the 3B1B intuition bar at all — define what "3B1B-grade" decomposes into as machine- or Alex-checkable acceptance criteria BEYOND no-clip/theme, and if the answer requires per-instance choreography the family template cannot carry, the real fix is a richer per-family instance-data contract (or motion/annotation layer), NOT a styling pass — otherwise you'll burn the loop repainting a ceiling that sits under the target.

## 📚 Domain Expert
AGENT: Domain Expert
STANCE: CONDITIONAL
REASONING: The architecture matches genuine prior art: ONE parametric renderer per family that every instance rides is exactly how VisuAlgo and Galles/USFCA ship — a single hand-tuned visualizer per algorithm class, never per-input bespoke art, which is precisely your verify-once scaling lever and the right call. Driving look at the family-renderer level with theme tokens + Storybook-style snapshot gating (your 1536×648/×730 + zero-overlap + token-conformance gate) is the standard design-system regression pattern and is sound. BUT the plan conflates two things prior art keeps separate: layout-correctness (no clip/overlap — what your gate checks) versus visual-intuition quality (3Blue1Brown grade), and 3B1B "intuition" does NOT come from a static SVG renderer at all — Manim's whole thesis is that meaning lives in MOTION/transitions (a value sliding into place, a partition wall sweeping), which a frozen-keyframe 480×360 SVG family cannot express, so a self-see loop on still frames will plateau at "clean, not magical." The self-see "render→look→repeat-back→confirm→edit" loop is the correct anti-mockup discipline (it mirrors how Bostock/D3 examples and Storywork converge), and Alex-as-aesthetic-driver is right given his content-vetting gap — approve all of that.
KEY CONCERN: The gate enforces non-overlap (layout correctness), not intuition; without an explicit per-family visual-intuition rubric (encoding legibility, motion/transition between keyframes, visual hierarchy, color semantics) that Alex signs off against, "shit except selection sort" will recur as "clean but flat" — the missing piece is animated transitions à la Manim/3B1B, which the static-SVG family contract currently has no slot for.

## ⚙️ Pragmatist

STANCE: CONDITIONAL
REASONING: The architecture is right — fixing look at the family-renderer level is the highest-leverage place to spend effort (one renderer, every instance lifts), and reusing the existing per-family visual gate + PM-delegation pattern means low net-new infrastructure cost. The real cost risk is not the build, it's the convergence loop: "Alex driving the aesthetic" via a human-eyeball cycle is exactly the `design_by_proliferation` failure mode wearing a process hat, and an ADHD single dev will burn out before convergence unless the loop is hard-bounded. The 2 missing families (timeline-protocol, state-machine) should be excluded from this pass — designing the look of a renderer that doesn't exist yet is speculative rework; redesign what's built, build the missing two to the now-locked aesthetic.
KEY CONCERN: The self-see loop has no machine-checkable termination condition — "Alex likes it" is a subjective oracle, and without a pre-committed visual target + a hard round-cap per family, this re-becomes the endless-mockup scar at full human-attention cost.

## 🧱 First Principles
AGENT: First Principles
STANCE: CONDITIONAL
REASONING: Stripped of framing, the actual goal is "a figure that makes THIS algorithm click for ONE ADHD visual learner" — and the dominant constraint is not scaling, it's that the corpus is LLM-generated and Alex can't vet content, which is precisely why typed-data-into-a-verified-renderer exists. From scratch I would NOT build per-concept bespoke 3B1B craft, because bespoke = un-gated = exactly the un-vettable surface the whole architecture was erected to forbid; a single dev with a free-LLM content pipeline cannot hand-craft motion per concept across 5 subjects and still trust it. The proposed approach is correctly shaped: 3B1B "click" is mostly not bespoke geometry but disciplined *visual grammar* (faithful encoding, motion that traces the actual state transition, restraint, focus) — and a family renderer is exactly where grammar lives, since a family is a class of algorithms that share a state-transition shape (compare/swap, partition, traverse). So "make the family renderer look good generically" is the right move ONLY if the design pass invests in transition-faithful motion and emphasis, not just static prettiness. The genuine risk is that the family granularity is too coarse: if one "sequence/array" renderer must serve selection sort AND merge AND quicksort, the generic version regresses to a lowest-common-denominator bar chart — which is what Alex already called "the old stuff."
KEY CONCERN: The deliverable must be a transition-aware visual GRAMMAR per family (motion/emphasis that animates the actual state-change the typed data already encodes), not a generic skin — and if a single family's instances don't share one transition shape, that's a signal to split the family, not to smear one renderer across mismatched concepts.

## ⚠️ Risk Analyst
AGENT: Risk Analyst
STANCE: CONDITIONAL
REASONING: The plan's fatal gap is that its only ship-gate (the mechanical visual gate) is orthogonal to the actual open problem (taste), so a renderer can pass CI green and still be "shit" — meaning the gate cannot stop the failure mode that motivated the work, and "only green CI gates ship" gives false assurance. The single taste oracle (Alex, ADHD, attention-scarce) is a hard bottleneck with no fallback: if his bar isn't frozen into a repeatable artifact (token-spec + golden reference screenshots + a rejected-examples gallery per family), every new family/instance and every LLM-picked instance re-litigates taste from zero, and the documented project failure mode (endless throwaway mockups + dev hand-doing) re-fires under the "self-see loop." Verify-once-per-family is a double-edged blast-radius lever: a good renderer lifts all instances, but a taste-drift or an instance-B-vs-instance-A mismatch silently degrades the whole family with no per-instance visual diff to catch it. The 2 non-existent families (timeline-protocol, state-machine/code-trace) have unproven look AND unproven data-shape, so their aesthetic risk is uncapped and shouldn't be promised on this pass. Mitigations that make this APPROVE-able: (1) capture Alex's verdict as a versioned golden-reference + token artifact per family so taste becomes a regression check, not a re-litigation; (2) add a perceptual/golden-image diff gate per instance (not just per family) to catch silent family-wide drift; (3) box this pass to the 4 existing families and treat the 2 missing families as a separate, later risk; (4) hard cap iterations per family to kill the throwaway-mockup spiral.
KEY CONCERN: HIGH — the only ship-gate is mechanical and orthogonal to the open problem (taste), so CI-green is not ship-readiness; combined with a single human taste-oracle whose bar isn't captured, the work risks shipping gate-green-but-"shit" renderers and re-litigating taste on every future family/instance.
