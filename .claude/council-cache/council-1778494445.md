## 🔴 Devil's Advocate

STANCE: REJECT
REASONING: The "Claude-in-chat fills cards per-task" plank is a self-deception that simply relocates the LLM-hallucination risk from a build-time pipeline to a session-time human bottleneck — and worse, it requires Alex to schedule a Claude session before he can study any of ~100 tasks across 5 subjects in a 3-4 week runway. The math is fatal: even at 15 minutes per task to curate four coherent cards against a glyph-mangled PDF, that's 25 hours of Claude-driven prep before Alex sees a complete card scaffold, on top of the 4 days of build. Meanwhile the REPL — the one surface that could carry Alex through finals without any cards at all — is gated behind R-package-install latency (VGAM/Cairo cold-compile 2-5 min on first run), a sandbox you haven't security-modelled for a VPS that also hosts the app, and base64-PNG plumbing that has never been smoke-tested end-to-end. You are shipping a card system that won't be populated, a REPL that may not execute, and calling it "ordered" because items 1-3 sound cheap. The hard rule "Alex never validates a card" is unenforceable without provenance metadata on the card row — which isn't in the schema task — so the first wrong Claude-curated card he hits is indistinguishable from a hallucination, and trust collapses on contact.
KEY CONCERN: Per-task Claude-in-chat curation is not a workflow, it's a deferred LLM dependency disguised as human curation, and it does not scale to 100 tasks in 3-4 weeks — ship the REPL+PDF+sidekick alone, drop the card schema entirely from Slice 2, and force the decision on whether cards are a real feature before reintroducing them.

## 📚 Domain Expert

STANCE: CONDITIONAL
REASONING: The proposed composition (verbatim PDF + multi-lang REPL + LLM sidekick on selection) is closest to **RStudio Cloud / Posit Cloud + Copilot Chat** for the PS R-script case and to **Replit Code Tutor** for POO C++ — both shipped patterns, both validated for "computational experiment workbench." It is NOT close to Khanmigo, Brilliant, or Codecademy CodeBytes, all of which presuppose authored curriculum that gates the chat surface. The slice therefore overfits to PS (R-simulation) and POO (compile-run); ALO (natural deduction / resolution proofs), SO+RC (protocol design + conceptual exam), and PA (algorithm + complexity proof) get a degraded experience — REPL is irrelevant for ALO/PA proof work, and sidekick-on-PDF-selection collapses to "ChatGPT with PDF context," which research (Kasneci et al. 2023; Mollick & Mollick 2023 on AI tutors) flags as high-hallucination on niche formal-logic and Romanian-curriculum-specific OS material. The "dormant 4-card schema" is genuine conceptual drift: DEF/WORKED/DRILL/CHECK is a **Cognitive Tutor / ASSISTments-style mastery-learning scaffold** (Anderson, Koedinger; ICAP "constructive" tier via DRILL self-explanation), while REPL+sidekick is an **exploratory/discovery-learning workbench** (ICAP "interactive/active" but unstructured) — Sweller's worked-example effect and Kalyuga's expertise-reversal effect both show novices with near-zero baseline lose 20-40% efficiency under discovery-mode UIs vs worked-example sequences, and Kirschner/Sweller/Clark 2006 ("Why Minimal Guidance Fails") is the load-bearing citation against pure ask-when-stuck for cramming novices. Sidekick-as-primary teaching surface fails the metacognition-gap test (Dunning-Kruger floor; Nelson & Narens 1990; Bjork's "desirable difficulties" require knowing what to retrieve) — Alex doesn't know what to ask on ALO resolution or SO scheduler invariants, so "tutor on tap" degrades to "Google with extra steps." The "Alex never validates a card" hard rule is correct for *card authoring* (Chi self-explanation evidence shows gains from consuming worked examples, not authoring them) but wrong if extended to *answer validation on DRILL/CHECK* — Roediger & Karpicke 2006 retrieval-practice + Dunlosky 2013 "Improving Students' Learning" meta-review both require the learner to commit an answer and receive feedback; removing that loop kills the testing effect. Claude-in-chat per-task authoring throughput at ~15-20 min/task × ~40 tasks across 5 subjects = 10-13 hours of authoring overhead, feasible in 21 days only if batched per subject in 2-3 sit-downs, not interleaved with study.
KEY CONCERN: Shipping REPL+PDF+sidekick while card schema stays dormant means Alex gets a discovery-mode workbench for ALL 5 subjects in the next 3 weeks — pedagogically inverted for a near-zero-baseline novice on ALO/SO/PA. Ship the slice ONLY if card-authoring is gated as a P0 blocker for ALO+SO+PA before any study session on those subjects (i.e., dormant schema becomes mandatory-pre-study for 3 of 5 subjects), and the "no validation" rule is narrowed to "Alex never validates *card quality*" (he MUST validate his own DRILL answers — that's the testing effect, not curriculum authoring).
