## 📚 Domain Expert
AGENT: Domain Expert
STANCE: CONDITIONAL
REASONING: The hybrid mechanical/judgment split, shape→grader routing, and two-read extraction are sound and align with how content pipelines and OCR-confidence ensembles work, but two design choices contradict established ITS theory. Single-shape-per-KC is fine as a *drill-format router* but you've conflated it with KC granularity: in Cognitive Tutor / ASSISTments / BKT, a KC is the atomic skill and a single problem routinely maps to MULTIPLE KCs — that's exactly why your "Problem has no kcId" fix must be `kcIds` (plural, which you have) AND why mastery must credit/blame all touched KCs, not one shape; the shape label belongs on the *problem/drill*, not as the KC's defining attribute. EWMA is a defensible, transparent stand-in but it is strictly weaker than the field standard: it has no guessing/slip parameters (BKT) and no spacing/decay term (PFA/AFM/DASH/FSRS-as-you-already-use), so a lucky guess and a known answer move mastery identically — at minimum gate the latch on item difficulty or borrow BKT's two-parameter guess/slip framing since you're already storing per-obs data. On grounding: "two independent reads + diff" is a legitimate and known-good *extraction-faithfulness* pattern (it's OCR ensemble / cross-read agreement, cousin to self-consistency), but it verifies PDF→text fidelity, NOT claim groundedness — the field's actual groundedness tools are NLI/attributed-QA/RAG-citation-verification, and diacritic-insensitive substring matching is the real landmine: in Romanian, stripping diacritics collapses distinct tokens and substring (vs span-anchored) matching will produce false-positive "grounded" hits against the wrong span, silently violating "no un-cited claim reaches a student."
KEY CONCERN: Diacritic-insensitive substring grounding is a false-positive generator — require span-anchored offsets into the real PDF text layer (not just _sources/*.md) with length/locality bounds, and treat the two-read diff as extraction-fidelity only, layered under a real NLI/attribution check for the KC-claim-to-source link.
