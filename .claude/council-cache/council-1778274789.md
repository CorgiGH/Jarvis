Prior-art mapping:

Bottleneck is time-on-task with the problem set, alone, no autocomplete. That's what a probabilities homework grades.

Citations on why WATCHING+APPLY is wrong primitive:
- Generation effect (Slamecka & Graf 1978, replicated): self-produced answers retain ~40% better than read answers.
- Cursor/Copilot longitudinal evidence: developers ship faster, retain less, measurable skill atrophy on cold-recall.
- Desirable difficulty (Bjork): smooth tools that remove friction degrade long-term retention even when they improve short-term performance.

A tool that watches the user's editor and offers fixes is the literal opposite of what a student preparing for closed-book exam needs. The whole point of doing PS by hand on paper IS the friction.
