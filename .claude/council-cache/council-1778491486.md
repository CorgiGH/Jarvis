## 📚 Domain Expert

AGENT: Domain Expert
STANCE: REJECT
REASONING: The verified locking design fundamentally misapplies Kapur's productive failure (2012, 2015), which requires students attempt a *target problem* then receive a *teacher-led canonical reveal with compare/contrast* — Kapur explicitly preserves access to prior conceptual grounding and treats the reveal as the consolidation phase, not a punishment unlock. Locking the DEFINITION card (conceptual primer, not solution) inverts this: novices on first contact need the schema, per Sweller/Kalyuga's expertise reversal effect (2003, 2010) where worked-examples-first dominates problem-solving-first for low-prior-knowledge learners — and Alex catching up across five courses three weeks from finals IS a Kalyuga-novice on Laplace MLE, not a Kapur intermediate with prerequisite schema. The right fix is Renkl & Atkinson's faded worked examples (2003): unlock DEFINITION + WORKED by default, keep CHECK·TRANSFER as the only gated capstone (true productive-failure surface), and let subsequent problems in the same concept fade steps — this is what ALEKS (Knowledge Space Theory), Khan Academy Mastery, and Brilliant actually ship; none expose a user-toggled "beginner mode" (rejecting proposals A and F) because adaptive ordering is response-data-driven, not self-declared. The two-mode UI is a workaround for a default-ordering bug; fix the default. On brutalism: Mayer's coherence principle favors less chrome (supports brutalist), but Pekrun's control-value theory ties learning affect to perceived competence — locked cards labeled "🔒 attempt drill first" actively signal *low control* on first paint, which is the real affective harm, not the monospace font; the reviewer is partially projecting on #5.
KEY CONCERN: Locking the DEFINITION card violates expertise reversal for a demonstrable novice and misreads Kapur — PF gates the *reveal* (worked solution), never the *primer*; unlock DEFINITION+WORKED by default, keep CHECK as the sole productive-failure gate.
