## 📚 Domain Expert
AGENT: Domain Expert
STANCE: APPROVE
REASONING: Swapping family-B from a second `:free` chatbot to a local NLI/entailment model is the textbook fix for the well-documented "self-consistency illusion" — two samples of the same model class are correlated, not independent, so their agreement carries near-zero added evidence (the exact failure SelfCheckGPT, FactCC, and the SummaC/AlignScore line of work were built to escape by using a *different-class* entailment scorer, not a second generation). MiniCheck specifically is the right reference: Tang et al. (2024) showed a 400M-770M fine-tuned checker matches GPT-4-level factual-consistency judgment at ~400x lower cost and runs CPU-feasible — directly addressing the RAM-starved VPS via your PC-side/serve-only split, which is itself the standard offline-batch + cached-verdict-serve pattern (not a request-path model load). The load-bearing UNCLEAR/abstain band (item 2) is also established practice — NLI three-way ENTAIL/CONTRADICT/NEUTRAL with a NEUTRAL→abstain floor is exactly how SummaC and AlignScore avoid the FactCC over-confidence trap, and your `Verdict.UNCLEAR`→non-agreement mapping already implements it correctly. On sequencing: building the verification spine BEFORE the teaching loop is correct here precisely because your product's *only* differentiated promise is "traceable to your lecture"; shipping the teaching loop first would mean shipping the unfalsifiable-trust failure mode (the FactCC/early-hallucination-detector lesson: a checker that can only say "looks fine" trains users to trust it, then breaks silently). Your engine cannot currently *say* faithful at all, so teaching has nothing truthful to gate on — B5 is the unblock, not a detour. APPROVE.
KEY CONCERN: The single thing that will actually break is the adapter contract (item 2), not the model choice — NLI models score a (premise, hypothesis) *pair*, and naive document-level entailment is the canonical SummaC failure (a 3-sentence quote vs. a 1-sentence claim under-entails and you get spurious NEUTRAL/CONTRADICT, flooring everything to uncertain a second time). Pin the contract to sentence/span-level granularity with the SummaC max-over-source-sentences aggregation, set the abstain threshold from a small labeled calibration set of YOUR KCs (not the model's default), and wire the coverage monitor (item 4) to distinguish "NONE-leg never ran" from "NLI ran but abstained" — otherwise you cannot tell a calibration miss from a genuine coverage gap, and you will have rebuilt the same blind "everything uncertain" wall one layer up.
