# Council review — 1781909887

**Problem:** Are two UX ideas good? (A) subject-focus mode ("only PA / only ALO"); (B) spoonfed/guided navigation instead of a multi-item menu.
**Project context:** jarvis-kotlin ADHD-first university tutor. Learning order = priority queue (exam-proximity × weight × readiness-gap × prereq, §7.4) + FSRS spaced repetition interleaving subjects, with a cross-subject interleave cap + INV-EC4.2 ("a goal cannot starve a real deadline"). `/oggi` (today queue) already = the intended funnel. `NextKcSelector.select()` already takes a `subject` filter param (line ~85). Nav = ~8 top items + bottom tabs.
**Timestamp:** 2026-06-20 (epoch 1781909887)

---

## 🔴 Devil's Advocate
IDEA A: CONDITIONAL — a hard one-subject filter violates the spec's own audit-P7 cross-subject interleave cap (§7.4 line 433) + the interleaving>blocking science, and can let a chosen subject STARVE a nearer real exam (the exact thing INV-EC4.2 forbids). Only admissible form = the existing EC4 goal: a priority BIAS a dated exam still outranks, never a flow-wide filter.
IDEA B: REJECT — `/oggi` already IS the spoonfed funnel; funnel + menu are meant to coexist. Stripping the nav amputates spec-mandated surfaces (/review FSRS due w/ live badge, /exam, /trust, /me) and collides with A (which is a demand for MORE steering).
KEY CONCERN: The two ideas contradict (A = "let me choose" / B = "stop making me choose"); the damaging one is A's filter semantics — the whole engine is system-owned cross-subject interleaving. Ship A only as EC4 goal-bias (bias, never filter; deadline always wins); keep B additive to nav, never subtractive.

## 🧱 First Principles
IDEA A: CONDITIONAL — subject-focus is a crude proxy for two already-spec'd needs: (1) "trust the queue preps my NEXT exam" = §7.4 deadline-aware priority (should be DEFAULT, not a mode); (2) "PA in 3 days, collapse the rest" = a time-boxed exam-crunch override EC4 already models + INV-EC4.2 protects. Approve a date-keyed crunch override; reject a persistent "only PA forever" toggle (lets an anxious ADHD student permanently dodge weak material).
IDEA B: CONDITIONAL — principle isn't "remove the menu," it's "one obvious next action, everything else ONE tap away (not zero, not twelve)." /oggi nails it; the 8-item nav contradicts it. Promote the single next-action + auto-advance; keep lateral access one tap (ADHD needs the escape hatch).
KEY CONCERN: Both ideas are one reframe: the SYSTEM decides what's next, but the decision must be legibly deadline-driven and always overridable in one tap. Build the §7.4 deadline-awareness and SURFACE its reasoning ("PA in 4 days, that's why these 6 are first") so the student trusts the queue instead of seizing the wheel.

## 📚 Domain Expert
IDEA A: CONDITIONAL — conflicts with interleaving transfer (Rohrer & Taylor 2007) but SDT autonomy (Deci & Ryan) is real — for ADHD, avoidance is often the bigger failure than suboptimal scheduling. Real tools keep interleaving as the ENGINE and scoping as a FILTER (Anki subdeck selection, Khan course focus). Approve only as a weighted "PA-weighted" filter that doesn't disable FSRS, NOT a "PA-only lockout."
IDEA B: APPROVE — guided/linear flows dominate ADHD-adapted tools because choice is extraneous cognitive load (Sweller CLT); Duolingo single-path, Cognitive Tutor, the "one primary action" pattern (Nielsen/Fitts). /oggi already encodes this at the data layer; the nav surface just hasn't committed. Convergent with evidence, not a tradeoff.
KEY CONCERN: A's risk is being built as a hard subject lock — must be a weighted filter, not a wall.

## ⚙️ Pragmatist
IDEA A: CONDITIONAL — the subject filter is ALREADY a first-class param on `NextKcSelector.select()` (line 85). Cheapest: persist a focus choice, pass it as the `subject` arg to `/api/v1/queue/today`, add a filter chip on OggiScreen. Hidden complexity: FSRS due dates don't care about subject → focusing skips other-subject overdue cards = a mastery-debt trap unless communicated ("review debt in ALO is accumulating").
IDEA B: CONDITIONAL — 80/20 = a routing default change: redirect `/` → `/oggi` when no pinned task + one prominent "Începe" CTA chaining oggi → lesson → workspace. Nav untouched. ~15 lines in App.tsx. A full wizard/nav-hide is much larger + unnecessary.
KEY CONCERN: A silently starves FSRS due cards in unfocused subjects — make it a soft session-level hint, OR show a cross-subject due-debt counter whenever focus is active.

## ⚠️ Risk Analyst
IDEA A: CONDITIONAL — footgun if it suppresses FSRS interleaving: lock PA during finals, ALO cards lapse past the retention cliff, other exam suffers (EC4 starvation). Acceptable only with all three: (1) filters the DISPLAY/queue surface only, never the scheduler weights; (2) persistent "X ALO cards overdue" banner even inside focus; (3) auto-expire after a session/24h. Without all three = CRITICAL; with = HIGH→MEDIUM.
IDEA B: CONDITIONAL — over-funneling collapses the escape hatch for the student who must override ("queue says ALO but PA exam in 3h"). Recoverable IF the funnel is a soft default with "show all"/override one tap away, not a locked rail. Verify /azi is reachable + populated FIRST (it's currently hard to reach).
KEY CONCERN: CRITICAL — naive sticky A that suppresses the scheduler can cause silent cross-subject FSRS lapse during finals, the window interleaving matters most, and the ADHD student is least likely to notice until after the exam. View-layer filter only; scheduler-touching focus = reject.
