# Council review — 1779987068

**Problem:** Is the core PRODUCT VISION for jarvis actually good — coherent, achievable, the right north star — or flawed/grandiose? Judge the idea itself, not implementation details.

**Proposed approach (the vision):** A tutor that is a "blank model" which ingests/digests ANY university subject's raw material (lectures, past exams) so deeply it can teach that subject like a professor — or BETTER — with strong illustration, personalized per student and their knowledge base. Decomposed: (1) deeply ingest ANY subject from material ALONE [keystone, mostly unbuilt]; (2) teach it well — illustrate + infer the right practice-style PER SUBJECT from the material; (3) personalize per student/knowledge-base. Load-bearing constraint: the builder (Alex) does NOT know the subjects and cannot supply subject expertise or per-subject pedagogy — the system must derive each subject's shape + teaching method automatically from the material.

**Project context:** Solo dev (Alex, CS undergrad building this to LEARN the subjects), for himself + a few classmates. Kotlin/Ktor + SQLite + React; single VPS; FSRS spaced-rep + drill grading + git-YAML content corpus already built; mid an 11-gate redesign (Gates 1-3 shipped). $0 LLM budget (OpenRouter free-tier + ClaudeMax relay only). Deep-ingestion/grounding ML pipeline (RAG, structured extraction) mostly UNBUILT; runtime = weak free models. Finals June 1-21 2026. Prior councils repeatedly flagged the owner using system-building as avoidance of studying.

## 🔴 Devil's Advocate

AGENT: Devil's Advocate
STANCE: REJECT
REASONING: The "blank model that deeply ingests ANY subject from material ALONE" is the keystone the entire vision rests on, and it is not merely unbuilt — it is a category error masquerading as a milestone. The one subject that exists (PA: 6 hand-authored KCs, 2 misconceptions, 1 lecture) was produced by `curate-tutor/SKILL.md`, where the skill itself says "Claude performs each stage" and "the human curator then reviews the YAML before committing." That is Alex-plus-Opus authoring content in an interactive Claude Code session — the most expensive, expert, non-scalable, non-free intelligence available — and the vision then asserts the product will do this autonomously on free-tier weaker LLMs at a runtime where Alex is absent. You cannot demote a task from "Opus with a human reviewing every quote for hallucination" to "free-tier model, unsupervised" and call it the same capability; the groundedness gate that makes the corpus trustworthy IS the human `git diff` review, and that gate vanishes at scale. Worse, the vision smuggles in an unprovable premise — that a subject's correct pedagogy is latent in its lecture text and machine-derivable — when the project's own evidence refutes it: Alex's single genuine pedagogical insight came from a human living the course a semester, not from parsing a PDF. If material-alone could yield the teaching method, Alex would have extracted his insight from the slides in week one.
KEY CONCERN: The keystone ("derive each subject's shape AND its correct pedagogy from material ALONE, autonomously, on free-tier LLMs") is silently being faked by a human + Opus authoring loop, and the vision has no theory for how the unsupervised, unpaid runtime closes that gap — grandiose by construction, not by execution. Every hour perfecting the authoring pipeline is an hour curating PA instead of studying it.

## 📚 Domain Expert

AGENT: Domain Expert
STANCE: CONDITIONAL
REASONING: The vision splits cleanly into one solved part and one unsolved-at-the-frontier part. Part (1)+(3) — ingest material into a knowledge-component graph (Bloom levels, prerequisite edges, misconception trigger/refutation pairs) and personalize via mastery tracking + spaced repetition — is exactly the proven Cognitive Tutor / ALEKS / Anki-FSRS architecture, and the corpus on disk shows it's real; that part earns approval. The grandiose part is keystone claim (2): "automatically DERIVE each subject's shape and the right teaching method from material alone." No deployed ITS does this — Cognitive Tutor's cognitive task analysis, ASSISTments' authoring, Khanmigo's pedagogy were all hand-built by domain experts and learning scientists, precisely the expertise Alex lacks; automated domain-model induction (SimStudent / Cen-Koedinger LFA) is a research problem, not a product feature. The spec itself does NOT attempt this — its pedagogy is a fixed 4-axiom / 5-phase rhythm applied uniformly to every KC, which quietly contradicts the "per-subject derived pedagogy" pitch. "Better than a professor at scale" is the standard edtech overclaim — Bloom's 2-sigma was a human result no automated system has reproduced (best ITS gains ~0.4-0.8σ).
KEY CONCERN: The load-bearing keystone (auto-derive per-subject pedagogy from material alone) is an open research problem the field hasn't solved, and the existing spec silently substitutes a single fixed pedagogy for all subjects — so either honestly downscope to "fixed research-backed pedagogy + auto-extracted content + personalization" (achievable, valuable), or it stays an unfalsifiable moonshot that conveniently justifies unlimited system-building.

## ⚙️ Pragmatist

AGENT: Pragmatist
STANCE: CONDITIONAL
REASONING: Pillars (2) and (3) are genuinely achievable and proportionate — deriving practice-style from content tags and personalizing via FSRS is heuristic-plus-LLM work a solo dev can ship, and the hard scaffolding (FSRS, drill grading, content corpus) already exists. But pillar (1), the keystone — "deeply ingest ANY subject from material ALONE so well it teaches like a professor or better" — quietly becomes a research program, attempted on free-tier/weak LLMs with an unbuilt RAG pipeline. The 90%-at-20% version: a competent material-grounded study aid (extract structure, generate graded drills, schedule recall, surface gaps) is hugely valuable and reachable; "better than a professor at any subject" is the grandiose 10% that costs 80% and won't be reached on this budget by June. The fatal practical detail: the builder can't supply subject expertise (so quality must come from the pipeline), but the pipeline runs on the weakest models (so quality is capped) — nobody competent is in the loop to catch when the blank model digests a subject wrong.
KEY CONCERN: With Alex unable to judge subject correctness AND ingestion on free-tier LLMs, there is no quality gate on whether the tutor actually understood the material — a confidently-wrong tutor is worse than no tutor for a first-time learner; scope pillar (1) down to "grounded, citation-backed, says 'I don't know'" and ship that, not the professor-beating ambition.

## ⚠️ Risk Analyst

AGENT: Risk Analyst
STANCE: CONDITIONAL
REASONING: The catastrophic-and-unrecoverable failure mode — a weak model silently fabricating subject content students can't detect — is CRITICAL in blast radius but already partially fenced. The committed corpus proves the mitigation is real: every KC/misconception carries a `source.doc` + verbatim `quote`, and `ContentValidator` (wired into `gradle check`) hard-fails any claim whose quote isn't a literal substring of the extracted source. That converts free-form hallucination into a bounded class (misattribution/wrong-emphasis of text that genuinely exists), with human-swipe approval as backstop. The residual CRITICAL risk: grounding only covers extraction — the citation-RAG / cross-family critic layers that would protect the generative teaching/illustration surface are entirely unbuilt and depend on the unbuilt Python sidecar, so when free-tier LLMs generate worked examples/narrative at runtime the unverifiable-error surface reopens. HIGH: scope-vs-correctness inversion — "ANY subject" + "better than a professor" + builder-can't-vet means correctness risk scales with the headline ambition, and $0/weak-model trades directly against the one thing (model quality) that reduces silent error.
KEY CONCERN: Sound and recoverable ONLY IF the verbatim-grounding + human-approval discipline already enforced at extraction is extended to gate every runtime-generated teaching artifact (worked examples, illustrations, narrative) before a student sees it — refuse-on-no-citation, not best-effort. Otherwise confident subtly-wrong content ships to the one audience structurally unable to catch it; at multi-subject scale, errors are discovered only by students failing exams. Acceptance criterion must be "no ungrounded claim reaches a student," not "the graph is well-formed."

VERDICT: FLAWED

CORE FINDING:
The idea splits in two and the council is unanimous on the split. The achievable, genuinely good core — auto-EXTRACT a subject's content into a knowledge graph + apply research-backed pedagogy + personalize via spaced-repetition/mastery — is proven prior art (Cognitive Tutor / ALEKS / Anki-FSRS) and you've already built much of it. The grandiose shell around it — "a blank model that digests ANY subject and teaches it BETTER than a professor, with pedagogy auto-DERIVED from raw material, on $0 weak models, validated by a builder who can't judge correctness" — stacks three unsolved research problems and an unfalsifiable quality claim. Two agents go further: that shell is the exact shape of the building-as-avoidance the prior councils already named, days before finals.

AGENT CONSENSUS: 2 REJECT, 3 CONDITIONAL — 0 flagged.

KEY ISSUES:
1. Auto-derived per-subject pedagogy (keystone claim #2) is an OPEN RESEARCH problem — no deployed ITS induces "which teaching method this subject needs" from raw material; all are hand-built by learning scientists. The spec already uses one fixed pedagogy for all subjects, contradicting the pitch. (Domain Expert, Devil's Advocate)
2. No quality gate. Builder-can't-vet + weak free-tier models = confident, subtly-wrong content shipped to students who also can't detect it; errors surface only when someone fails an exam. (Pragmatist, Risk Analyst, Devil's Advocate)
3. "Better than a professor" is an overclaim — best automated tutors land ~0.4-0.8σ, not Bloom's human-tutor 2σ. Defensible north star = "as good as a well-instrumented, personalized study tool." (Domain Expert)
4. Displacement risk: the universal-tutor framing conveniently justifies unlimited building over the actual goal — passing finals in subjects you don't yet know, starting June 1. (First Principles, Devil's Advocate)

RECOMMENDED PATH:
Keep the idea as a long-term north star — downscoped and honest. (a) Reframe to "material-grounded, personalized study tool that auto-EXTRACTS content and applies a FIXED research-backed pedagogy," NOT "auto-derives pedagogy + beats professors." Per-subject tuning = lightweight heuristics from existing tags (Bloom level, proof-vs-fact), not pedagogy induction. (b) Make the non-negotiable acceptance gate "no un-cited claim reaches a student — refuse / say 'I don't know' over best-effort," extending the verbatim-grounding you already enforce at extraction to every runtime-generated teaching artifact. (c) Respect the calendar: this is a post-finals build. Until June 21, the highest-value use of the tool is the part that already works — drilling the PA cards you have. Don't let designing the universal version replace studying.

CONFIDENCE: 8
What would change it: a strong runtime model (not free-tier) materially lowers the correctness risk; and if finals weren't imminent, the displacement concern — the main thing separating FLAWED from APPROVED — largely evaporates.
